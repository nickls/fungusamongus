# Algorithm v0.5.0

**Commit:** `ca15d44` | **Date:** 2026-04-20

## Summary

Major restructure to 6-factor model with per-day scoring (days 0-7). Split temperature into soil threshold + warming trend as separate factors. Added SPA frontend. First version with proper unit tests (59).

## Key characteristics

- **6-factor model:** soil threshold (25), warming trend (25), moisture (20), burn quality (15), sun/aspect (10), air temp (5)
- **Per-day scoring:** each burn scored independently for days 0-7 using day-specific weather windows
- **Linear regression** for warming trend (replaced the noise-prone half-split comparison)
- **Soil gate applies to ALL factors** — not just warming trend
- **Old burns get zero:** if recency > 30 months, type+size bonus not awarded
- **SPA frontend:** Leaflet map with day picker, filter sliders, heatmap, sparkline popups

## Factor weights rationale

Moisture is the gate (30→20), soil temp is the trigger (25+25=50 of the temperature system). Burn quality reduced from 35→15 because the temperature system should dominate timing. Sun/aspect split out as its own factor (10) for local optimization.

## What changed from v0.4.0

- Split from 4 factors to 6 factors
- Temperature split into soil threshold (25) + warming trend (25) as independent factors
- Linear regression slope for trend instead of half-split mean comparison
- Per-day scoring with `make_day_weather()` windowing
- Soil gate now crushes ALL factors when soil is below threshold
- Burn quality: old burns (>30mo) get zero including type/size bonus
- Added 59 unit tests covering all factors, cold snaps, and predictive scenarios

## Known issues (fixed in v0.6.0)

- Warming trend used only 14-day forecast soil temps — no historical soil data
- No cumulative degree-day calculation (research shows GDD is more predictive than trend)
- Single-point aspect sampling — one centroid for a 71-acre burn is misleading
- Static aspect scoring — south=5 regardless of month (should shift through season)
- No rain event count — literature shows count of >10mm events matters, not just total precip
- Soil temp threshold at 48F was too conservative (research shows 43F onset)
