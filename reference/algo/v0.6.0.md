# Algorithm v0.6.0

**Commit:** `ef1c1fc` | **Date:** 2026-04-20

## Summary

Research-driven update incorporating findings from peer-reviewed morel ecology studies. Replaced linear warming trend with cumulative soil growing degree-days (GDD). Added historical soil temp from archive API, multi-point aspect sampling, month-adjusted aspect scoring, and rain event counting.

## Key characteristics

- **6-factor model:** soil threshold (25), **soil GDD** (25), moisture (20), burn quality (15), sun/aspect (10), air temp (5)
- **Soil GDD replaces warming trend:** cumulative degree-days above 32F over 30-day history + 14-day forecast. Literature: 365-580 GDD predicts onset.
- **Historical soil temp:** archive API now fetches `soil_temperature_0_to_7cm` hourly data for 30-day GDD calculation
- **Multi-point aspect:** 5 elevation samples per burn (center + 4 cardinal at 150m). Scores using the most south-facing aspect found.
- **Month-adjusted aspect:** April: south=5, north=0. July: south=2, north=3. South-facing advantage shrinks as season progresses.
- **Rain event count:** bonus for 2+ precipitation events >0.4in (10mm) in 30 days (literature-supported)
- **Lower soil thresholds:** ideal 45-58F (was 48-58F), gate at 38F (was 40F), onset at 43F (was 45F)

## Research basis

From `reference/predictive-modeling-for-burn-scar-morel-mushrooms.md`:

- Soil temp onset at 43F (6.1C) — French field studies, USDA synthesis
- 365-580 cumulative soil GDD above 0C predicts emergence — Missouri 5-year study
- Rain events >10mm in prior 30 days increase abundance — Missouri field study
- South-facing slopes fruit first, advantage diminishes through season — USDA synthesis
- 3-4 week pre-emergence lag — French development studies

## Factor weights rationale

Unchanged from v0.5.0. The GDD replaces warming trend at the same weight (25) but measures accumulated heat rather than trend direction. This is more biologically grounded — morel primordia respond to total heat accumulation over weeks, not to whether today is warmer than yesterday.

## What changed from v0.5.0

- `warming_trend` → `soil_gdd` (cumulative degree-days, not linear regression slope)
- Historical soil temp fetched from Open-Meteo archive API (`soil_temperature_0_to_7cm`)
- Soil temp thresholds lowered ~3-5F per literature
- Multi-point aspect (5 samples per burn, best used for scoring)
- Month-adjusted aspect weights (configurable per month)
- Rain event count >0.4in bonus in moisture scoring
- `get_best_aspect()` in elevation.py replaces `get_slope_aspect()` in morel_finder

## Known limitations

- GDD thresholds (365-580) are from Missouri, not Sierra Nevada — may need regional calibration
- No field report feedback loop yet to validate scores against actual finds
- Surface burn fraction (>50% threshold from Yosemite study) not captured in data
- Cumulative degree-day base of 32F (0C) — some literature suggests 43F (6C) base may be better for morel-specific GDD
